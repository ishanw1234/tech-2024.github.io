    History of AI
    Early Beginnings (Before 1950s):

    The seeds of AI were sown in ancient cultures with stories of artificial beings like Pygmalion's Galatea. Philosophers like Aristotle pondered the nature of intelligence and whether it could be replicated mechanically.

    Birth of AI (1950s):

    The development of digital computers in the 1940s laid the foundation for modern AI research. Alan Turing, a pioneer in computer science, proposed the Turing Test in 1950 as a benchmark for machine intelligence. In 1956, John McCarthy coined the term "artificial intelligence" at the Dartmouth Workshop, a historic event that marked the official launch of AI research.

    Early Optimism and Challenges (1960s-1970s):

    The 1960s saw a surge in enthusiasm for AI, with researchers making significant progress in areas like game playing and theorem proving. However, limitations in computing power and the complexity of human intelligence led to a period of disillusionment in the 1970s, known as the "AI Winter." Resurgence and Progress

    (1980s-Present):

    The development of more powerful computers and the rise of machine learning techniques like neural networks revitalized AI research in the 1980s. Today, AI is experiencing a new golden age. AI applications are transforming various industries, from healthcare and finance to transportation and entertainment.

    The Future of AI:

    As AI continues to evolve, there is ongoing debate about its potential impact on society. Researchers are exploring ways to develop safe and ethical AI systems that can benefit humanity.

    Chat GPT (GPT-4)
    Chat GPT is one of the most versatile and popular LLMs that started the AI Boom. It was launched in November 2022 by OpenAI and has attracted billions of users since its release. It offers a simple UI, insane creativity and is one of the most stable model so far. It has both, a Paid Model(GPT-4) as well as a Free Model(GPT-3.5) and the paid model comes with DALL-E Browsing which enables image generation. You can use it for writing poems for your loved ones, planning events, writing simple codes, getting amazing recipes from dishes all around the world, getting pickup lines and what not!! It is used by hundreds of other AI tools as its base model. Prompt engineering can be used to get amazingly well results like a Professional, Friendly, Casual or a Humourous tone and the introduction of Internet Connectivity has opened its doors to the new information happening all around the world as well.     Gemini (Bard)
    Gemini (previously known as Bard) is an LLM introduced by Google developed by their DeepMind Research Faculty. It was launched by March 21, 2023 but didn't get much hype until Feb 2024 when it was re-branded to Gemini and Google made some big promises about their New-Gen AI Model. Like ChatGPT, it comes in two variants, one being paid and the other one being free. It also offers free image generation but as of April 2024 it is not very reliable. It is one of my favourite LLM but it does give incorrect information sometimes so it is not as reliable as ChatGPT but excels at giving factual answers rather than GPT which is known for its creative answers. It has realtime access to Internet even in its free variant which gives it an edge and the best alternative to Chat GPT. Gemini has also been embedded in Gmail, Google Docs and in almost all Google Products to increase ease of access. It is available at gemini.google.com and get more information about Gemini here -->
    Perplexity
    Perplexity is a new AI-Powered LLM that's shaking things up. It utilizes natural language processing to understand your search intent and provide comprehensive answers, not just keyword-stuffed links. It prioritizes credible sources with citations and delivers up-to-date information. You can even ask follow-up questions within the same search for a more interactive experience. With a freemium model and mobile app availability, Perplexity offers a promising alternative to traditional search engines and other LLM's like GPT and Gemini. It also has an amazing feature that lets you add a lot more words than ChatGPT and Gemini by converting it to txt and it can be useful to get information about a specific text that's large enough to not be put entirely at once in GPT or Gemini. It is available at perplexity.ai and get more information about Perplexity here -->
